What you should do when you receive a suspicious email

1
Stop — do not click any links
Urgency is a manipulation tactic. Real banks and service providers do not demand immediate action via email or SMS.
2
Verify the sender's email address carefully
Hover over the "From" name to reveal the real address. Look for misspellings like mukuru-alerts.com vs mukuru.com.
3
Go directly — never click, always type
If you need to check your account, open a new browser tab and type the official URL manually. Never click links in emails.
4
Report it to your IT or security team
Forwarding suspicious emails to your IT department helps protect everyone in your organization, not just yourself.
5
Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
Even if your password is stolen, 2FA means attackers cannot access your account without your phone. Enable it everywhere you can.
